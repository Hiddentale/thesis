Has 12 pages filled with dense theorems/proofs, definitely enough material to write about especially since this paper is
from 2021 and has open questions at the end. Hence there might be follow-ups.

Notation for turan number: ex(n, H) means that we try to find the maximum possible(as in # of edges) graph of n vertices,
where no subgraph is isomorphic to H.

2-degenerate means that if we keep removing vertices of degree 2 or less from the graph, we eventually succesfully
remove all vertices and no graph is left. If we eventuallyget a non-empty subgraph where every vertex is of degree
3 or higher, our initial graph is not 2-degenerate.

So basically, the conjecture that is being disproved in this paper is that:
If our bipartite H-graph is 2 degenerate, then the maximum number of edges we can have on our n-vertices graph,
where no subgraph can be exactly the same as H, has a worst-case bound of O(n^{3/2}) and vice-versa.

The paper only disproves the only if direction: If not 2-degenerate --> ex(n, H) = w(n^{3/2})
They give a counterexample bipartite H that is not 2-degenerate yet it still satisfies ex(n, H) = O(n^{3/2})
